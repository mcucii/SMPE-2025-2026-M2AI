## Quiz 2
### A Historical Overview of Note-Taking

1. What is the origin of the codex?

    Right answer:  A "struggle" for cultural hegemony between Ptolemy in Alexandria and Eumenes from Pergamon.

    Previously, scrolls were used, and the trasition to codices came from two reasons. First one is that people started putting together their tablets, making notebooks. And the second reason was that there was a struggle for cultural hegemony between two heirs. They competed in trying to build the best library. Then, one of them prohibit the exportation of papyruses,which was only made in Egypt. Than, the other heir found the solution - he started creaing codices from goat or sheep skin, that was then processed so it could be written on it.


2. What aspect of Eusebius work is presented in this sequence?

    Right answer: His canon tables (cross-references between the Gospel books) 

    Eusebius wrote many books on the history of Christianity. He realized that the Gospels were not identical and based on that he wrote Eusebian Canons, where he wanted to make it easier to compare different Gospels of Mark, Matthew, Luke and John. He made first example of a cross reference - numbering different parts from Gospels, writing down Gospels that were common to all four of them, and considered passages which were common only for three of them, and so on, for every combination. 


3. In which line should the keyword "Analysis" go in John Locke's index ?

    Right answer:   « Aa » 

    John Locke's index contains a line for every letter of the alphabet and a subdivision along the lines of vowels. The goal was to write down the pages where the keywords are used
    by using the first letter of the keyword and its first vowel. So, for the word "Analysis", first letter is "A", and first vowel is "a", so we should look for it in section "Aa".
